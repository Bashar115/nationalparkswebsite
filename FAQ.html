FAQ

Q: How do I get to Yosemite? A: Keeping in mind that GPS units often provide incorrect driving directions in and around Yosemite, please take a look at our driving directions.

Q: Are there vehicle restrictions in Yosemite? A: Some roads have vehicle restrictions on height, length, and/or width.

Q: Can I drive my own vehicle into the park? A: You can drive your own vehicle into and around Yosemite. Shuttle buses are not mandatory, but are recommended (except in 2021, when they are not available).

Q: Will I need to bring tire chains? A: Tire chains may be required in fall, winter, and spring due to snowy or icy conditions. It's not possible to predict when exactly they may be required since requirements are based on conditions.

Q: What is the best time to visit Yosemite National Park? A: The best time to visit Yosemite is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the park's natural beauty is at its peak. Summer is also popular, but it can be crowded.

Q: How do I make camping reservations in Yosemite? A: Camping reservations can be made on the official Yosemite National Park website or by calling the National Park Service reservation line. Reservations are highly recommended, especially during peak seasons.

Q: Are there hiking trails suitable for beginner in Yosemite? A: Yes, Yosemite offers a range of hiking trails suitable for beginners. The Valley Loop Trail and Lower Yosemite Falls are great options with stunning views and manageable difficulty.

Q: Can I see wildlife in Yosemite National Park? A: Yes, Yosemite is home to diverse wildlife, including black bears, mule deer, and various bird species. Visitors are advised to maintain a safe distance and not feed the wildlife.

Q: What is the Half Dome Hike, is a permit required? A: The Half Dome hike is a challenging but rewarding trek. A permit is required to ascend the cables on Half Dome. Permits can be obtained through a lottery system.
